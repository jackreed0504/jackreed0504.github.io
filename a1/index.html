  5. What is your impression of the intended primary goal of the interactive experience?

    I think the intended goal of the experience is to advertise a Richard Mille watch. The introduction describes the experience as “A tribute to the grand unveiling of the RM 07-01 coloured ceramics”.

  6. How does the interactive experience communicate this primary goal?

    The experience uses interactive digital media techniques to immerse the user into a series of animated scenes which evoke the design aesthetics of the advertised watch. Reflecting the bright colours and retro design aesthetics of the Memphis design influenced watch, the animations depict vibrant images of summertime and beaches, imitating the look of lithographic printing and mid-20th century travel posters. This choice not only communicates the aesthetic of the watches but plays into the audience’s emotions of nostalgia to interest them in the product.

    The interactive elements include panning around the scenes, pressing and/or holding certain areas to trigger animations and sounds, and pressing buttons to play poetry. These immerse the viewer into the advertisement and scenes, ensuring their full attention, and reinforcing the emotional impact of the nostalgic scenery.

    The user can drag and spin a wheel with different nodes/dots to view different scenes. Numbers at the bottom of the screen seem to represent the time, scenes transitioning from bright blue, early morning to orange and red sunset. The use of the circular control seems to represent the imagery of a watch. The use of the watch imagery and cycling through time via retro imagery conveys a sense of an emotional importance of watches and time to the viewer.

    Once the user reaches the end, a button saying “Discover Your Journey” takes the viewer to an advertisement of the watches itself, with buttons linking to the Richard Mille site for the watch.

    The use of interactive animations and poetry to tell a story helps communicate Richard Mille’s brand identity. Richard Mille watches are a luxury item known for their groundbreaking designs. The use of this interactive storytelling medium tells communicates the idea that the watch is deeply meaningful and important, justifying its luxury price point. Furthermore, the use of a modern and non-traditional form of advertising conveys the idea that Richard Mille watch designs are groundbreaking.

  7. What is your impression of how the experience should be interacted with over time? (For how long and how many different times)

    It seems to be designed to be interacted with once. I would imagine a user would probably not be expected to spend longer than 10 minutes with the experience.

  8. How does the interactive experience communicate how it should be interacted with over time?

    The interaction is mostly linear (though the user can go backwards and forwards through the scenes) and gives one outcome, so there is little replay value. As it is an advertisement for a product, and web based, it is unlikely the experience will be accessible and live on the web for longer than the product is for sale.

    The encouragement of the user to explore, pan around and interact with the scenes suggests they should invest a reasonable amount of time and attention to the experience. The use of poetry, storytelling and animations communicates that this is an art piece and demands attention and careful admiration from the viewer. - If a user interacts with everything in the experience, I estimate that it would take around 5 to 10 minutes.

  9. What other media forms (digital or otherwise) does the experience reference?

    The art style of the animations references mid-20th century travel posters through the beach imagery, bright solid colours and an appearance which imitates lithographic printing.

    The experience features poetry which is displayed along with the animations.

    The experience references the watches it is advertising in several ways: the bright colours which recall the watches’ Memphis Group influenced design, the RM 07-01 watch is directly mentioned and advertised at the beginning and end, and through the circular time control used to view different scenes.

  10. What does this reference/s communicate to you about how you should act when engaging with your research experience?

    The use of the poetry and travel poster influenced imagery communicates to the viewer that they should take time to explore the scenery and admire the images as artworks and storytelling rather than as an advertisement.

    The direct references to the watch’s name, by contrast, communicate to me that the experience is an advertisement, and shouldn’t be analysed as someone’s personal expression.

    The use of the watch imagery in the circular control communicates that you are using it to explore through linear time and encourages you to progress time forward to experience the complete story.

  11. What does this reference/s communicate to you about how you should feel when engaging with your research experience?

    The use of the retro travel poster influenced imagery communicates that the viewer should feel emotions of nostalgia, as well as the feelings of relaxation and pleasure associated with vacations.

    The nostalgic feeling is reinforced by the watch imagery and time controls, evoking the connection between emotions and time.

    The experience aims to connect these emotions with the product, making users feel compelled to purchasing it.

  12. What is the most frustrating part of the interaction to you and what makes that part frustrating?

    When a scene has an interactive (glowing) element, there is no indication of how it should be interacted with (should it be pressed or held? will it make a sound or animation?). This makes the experience confusing, as it is not always clear whether you are making the correct interaction or getting the intended feedback.

  13. What is the most satisfying part of the interaction to you and what makes that part satisfying?

    The most satisfying part of the interaction for me is the ability to pan the scenes. This allows users to admire and explore the artwork and take the experience at their own desired pace.
